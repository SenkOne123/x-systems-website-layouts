What is forex trading

The international financial market where currency exchange takes place is called Forex, from the word "foreign exchange".

Forex is essential for the smooth operation of the world economy and ensures the capital flow between different countries. Central banks of different countries, international companies, commercial banks and traders take part in Forex trading.

Forex trading can be done from anywhere in the world - the only requirements are a good Internet connection and access to the foreign exchange market. Access to the market is provided via a broker's trading platform.

The idea of making money in the Forex market lies in playing on exchange rates. Moreover, it is possible to earn on both increase and decrease of rates. To put it simply, you can either buy cheaper and sell more expensive or sell the currency at a higher price and buy it back when the price goes down.

Currency rates are influenced by many different factors: economic indicators and policies of national banks, political changes, force majeure such as natural disasters, major technological accidents, terrorist attacks and even weather conditions, as well as rumors, sentiments and expectations of market participants. Despite this, the Forex market is notable for its relative stability because the fall of one currency always entails a change in the rates of the other. Professional traders use this to earn money.

In contrast to the stock exchanges, the Forex market operates 24 hours a day, 5 days a week, since there are agents buying and selling currency in each time zone during their working day. The trading activity of different currency pairs follows the time zones.

Forex trading in Hong Kong

Is forex trading legal in Hong Kong?

The autonomous region within the People's Republic of China, Hong Kong is one of the largest investment centers and financial markets. And no doubt Forex trading in Hong Kong is a legitimate way to earn money. Moreover, Forex trading is quite an attractive investment opportunity for local residents. The financial regulator here is the HK SFC - Hong Kong Securities and Futures Commission.

The HK SFC is dedicated to strengthening and protecting the integrity and sustainability of Hong Kong's securities and futures markets for the benefit of investors and the industry through the Securities and Futures Ordinance (SFO) and regulations. The Hong Kong Securities and Futures Commission also regulates and supervises foreign currency brokers who wish to establish their brokerage business in the country.

Taxes on forex trading in Hong Kong

Hong Kong has a territorial taxation system. In this system, incomes of both residents and non-residents are taxed only if their sources are within the Hong Kong territory or jurisdiction. So even residents do not pay taxes on incomes earned outside the country. In Hong Kong there is no tax on capital gains, dividends and interest. This is the major attraction for Forex traders and promotes the popularization of this type of earnings.

Forex trading software in Hong Kong

Forex deals are associated with the risk of capital loss. A trader who is able to analyze the market is better protected from financial losses than a trader who relies on intuition. To analyze the current market situation professional traders use special forex trading apps and forex trading software that provide access to the business news, market prices data and tools for tracking and forecasting. The necessary instruments for real-time market evaluation that you can use when trading forex in Hong Kong are provided in the analytical tool xSignals.

Forex trading signals and bots in Hong Kong

Forex trading signals as an analytical tool as well as their use are absolutely legal in Hong Kong. They allow beginners to grasp the mechanics of Forex market quickly and professionals to optimize trading and increase their productivity.

Trading signals are forecasts of asset price movements based on technical and fundamental analysis. Using trading signals a trader doesn't need to make complex calculations and deeply analyze the market on his own.

In addition to forex signals, some traders use trading robots. A trading robot is software that places orders according to certain developers' algorithms, trading strategies and settings which you can adjust. The advantage of robots is their quick reaction to market changes, which is not always available to a common person.

However, it is important to understand that there are no 100% effective trading robots. Moreover, not all brokers allow using trading robots on their platform. So when choosing a trading robot you need to clearly understand what algorithms, technical indicators and tools the chosen robot uses, whether they match your trading strategy and whether your broker allows the use of robots on its platform.

Forex trading tips

1. Choosing a broker

Choose a reputable broker regulated by HK SFC. When choosing a broker, learn about possible fees, commissions and charges. Otherwise, you may lose a significant portion of your profits.

2. Trading strategy

Before you start trading, you need to determine how you will make trading decisions and what information you'll need to enter or exit a trade. Be consistent and make sure that your trading strategy matches the changing dynamics of the market.

3. Trader's diary

Keep a record of all trades and analyze them using a trading diary. Make notes of all factors influencing your decisions. This will allow you to gain control and discipline, and make trading as consistent as possible.

4. Use a demo accoun

This is a training account with virtual money. Using a demo account you can learn how to conduct operations on the Forex market, learn popular trading strategies and test your own, begin to navigate in the nuances of currency speculations, assess the convenience of the trading platform under fire.

The Forex market does not tolerate amateurs. If you want to be successful, you must acquire special knowledge before you start trading. Learn all the time. Forex market as a dynamic system and requires a proper approach. You must be ready to adapt to fast-changing market scenarios and world events.

Forex transactions are made through computer networks among traders around the world, not on one centralized exchange. You can miss out on investment opportunities that rarely show up with a poor internet connection.

Choose a reliable broker, register on its trading platform, open an account and make a deposit. When choosing a broker, find out what the minimum deposit is. If you are a novice trader, use a micro forex trading account with low deposit requirements.

Use special applications and various tools to develop and validate your trading strategy. Test it on your broker's demo account.

Forex trading involves a high risk of losing capital, so use only the funds you can really afford to lose. Consider the leverage you can take. Do not engage in trades with high leverage if you are only starting out. Having realistic expectations of what you can do will help you be more successful.

Use stop-losses and take-profits to manage your trading risk. At the end of the day, check your positions and make sure you have no open contracts that need to be closed and that you have enough money in your account for future operations.
