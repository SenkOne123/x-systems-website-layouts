What is crypto trading

Exploiting the digital currency price movements is called crypto-trading or trading on a cryptocurrency exchange. This type of trading involves exchanging one cryptocurrency for another, buying and selling tokens, as well as exchanging fiat money for cryptocurrencies.

Unlike traditional currency and stock markets, the cryptocurrency market has high volatility and low liquidity. This means that coin values can change rapidly within a short time span. At the same time, a crypto trader can profit not only from an increase in the market value of the asset but also from a decrease.

High volatility provides both high profitability and equally high risks. To become a successful crypto trader, you should have a wide variety of information - from the actual prices of cryptocurrencies to the development potential of a particular coin.

Cryptocurrency exchanges operate 24/7. Industry professionals choose a convenient time for crypto trading according to their trading strategy.

Сryptocurrency trading in Hong Kong

Сryptocurrency trading in Hong Kong

Cryptocurrency and crypto trading are legal in Hong Kong and regulated by the Securities and Futures Commission (SFC).

Cryptocurrencies are considered virtual commodities or securities in Hong Kong, not legal tender. Currently, there are no clear rules for private ownership or transfer of cryptocurrency between individuals, as long as the tokens are received and transferred in good faith and do not violate anti-money laundering laws.

And while there is currently no specific legislation aimed at regulating cryptocurrency in Hong Kong, the SFC regulates anyone involved in cryptocurrency trading that falls under the SFO definition of "securities" or "futures contracts," requiring them to be licensed.

Taxes on crypto trading in Hong Kong

There is no tax on capital gains, dividends or interest in Hong Kong. However, professional cryptocurrency trading is treated as a regular business. In this case, the income from crypto trading is subject to income tax in Hong Kong. The maximum level of income tax is currently 16.5%.

The taxation associated with initial coin offerings (ICOs) depends on whether the ICO is treated as an offering of securities or as a futures or service/commodity exchange contract.

The cryptocurrency tax is extremely complex and is constantly changing in Hong Kong, as the regulation in this field is still in its formative stages. Use your tax advisors to make sure you are complying with all the necessary tax requirements.

Crypto trading tips

1. Use a licensed broker

By choosing a licensed broker, you protect yourself from financial crimes and shady brokers.

2. Don't focus on affordability.

Don't buy a coin just because its price is low. The decision to buy a coin should depend on its market capitalization. The higher the market capitalization, the more suitable the coin is for investing.

3. Diversify your cryptocurrency portfolio

Since cryptocurrency is unpredictable, investing in different coins will help you avoid large losses. A drop in one cryptocurrency always entails a change in the rate of another.

4. Develop your own crypto trading strategy

You might need to study a lot of literature, spend hours analyzing the market and so on. But having a clear plan will allow you to avoid market noise and get a small but sure profit.
