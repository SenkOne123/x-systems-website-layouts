What is crypto trading

As in any type of trading, the main goal in crypto trading is to make a profit from buying or selling cryptocurrency. The foundation of trading digital coins lies in correctly predicting which direction the price of your chosen cryptocurrency pair will go and then placing the necessary order. In this regard, it is important to understand how the cryptocurrency trading market operates in addition to using the various orders available which can help to reduce your risk.

The main feature of the cryptocurrency market is its high volatility and low liquidity. High volatility occurs in low liquid markets during times of significant trade activity. This means that players with large capital carry out large purchases or sales, which can result in a severe change in cryptocurrency rates. It is also an advantage of the crypto market since high returns can be extracted, but there are risks involved. Stock and foreign exchange markets are better protected from the point of view of legislation, but they do not offer the same flexibility and asset diversity as the cryptocurrency market.

What time is better to trade?

Cryptocurrency exchanges are open 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. Their liquidity can vary and usually this depends on the time of the trading day. Choosing the best time to trade cryptocurrency depends on the trader’s trading strategy, but it is always best to make trades at the same time.

Cryptocurrency trading in the US

Is crypto trading legal in US

There is a fairly complex attitude towards the regulation of cryptocurrency in the US, and this is primarily due to the country’s legal system. Although both federal and state law exists in the US, there is no uniform policy regarding the status of crypto in different states as well as no clear legal regulation of cryptocurrency. At the federal level, the Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (hereinafter FinCEN) possesses the competency to regulate cryptocurrency activities, except issues related to taxation.

Taxes on crypto trading
in the US

Since 2014, cryptocurrency has been treated as property and the corresponding taxation rules are applicable to it. Currently, all crypto transactions are subject to capital gains tax or personal income tax. Tax issues in the US are the responsibility of the Internal Revenue Service. Tax rates are dependent on the state in which the company is registered. When carrying out activities related to exchanging or selling cryptocurrencies, entities are required to adhere to the rules of combatting money laundering and KYC requirements. The Internal Revenue Service also oversees existing licensed exchanges and cryptocurrency exchanges. An exchange or exchange service will be contested if they operate without a license or authorization. The transfer of funds from cryptocurrencies to fiat currencies are of greatest interest and it is precisely these transfer points that are most closely monitored by the IRS.
